Makara-Saṅkrānti. This is a very important religious observance and festival. About 70 years ago (in the author's boyhood) it occurred on the 12th or 13th January according to the several Indian almanacs then cuğrent, but now it falls, owing to the procession of the equinoxes, on the 13th or the 14th of January in the month of Pausa. In modern times very few, if at all, go through the prc cedure prescribed even in the Dharmasindhu. A fast is observed and Siva is worshipped and people may listen to stories of Siva's exploits. Common people in some parts drink a beverage called bhāng prepared by pouring water on crushed hemp leaves with the addition of almonds, rose leaves, opium seeds &c. It is supposed to be dear to Saṅkara. In many temples of Siva, water is made to drip on the linga incessantly.
