тАШYamas,тАЩ тАШRestraints,тАЩ are negative, of the nature of prohibitions; e.g., тАШthe Br─Бhmaс╣Зa should not be killed,тАЩ тАШwine should not be drunk,тАЩ and so forth. тАШNot necessarily the Observances.тАЩтАФThis does not mean that one shall not keep the Observances; all that is meant is that the Restraints are more obligatory in their character than the Observances.

This is what is further emphasized:тАФтАШNot observing the Restraints, one falls.тАЩ If the Restraints are not observed, it means that Br─Бhmaс╣Зa-killing and such acts are done, which means that the man has become an outcast, and people do not find it advisable to sit near him or have any dealings with him. It is not so in the case of the non-keeping of the Observances. To this effect we have the following assertions current among cultured people:тАФ

тАШThe man who keeps the Observances, but is not intent upon the Restraints, falls; but he who observes the Restraints, but not the Observances, does not suffer. Therefore, one should devote oneтАЩs attentions to the Restraints, not minding the Observances so much.тАЩ
