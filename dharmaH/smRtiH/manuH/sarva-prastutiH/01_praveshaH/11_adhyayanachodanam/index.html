Adhyetavyam-pravaktovyamтАЩтАФтАШcan be studied and can be taughtтАЩтАФthe verbal affix denotes capability, not injunction [ i.e. the meaning is that the institutes deserve to be studied and taught &c.]; for actual injunctions are going to begin only from the Second Discourse onward; and the present Discourse is purely descriptive, it contains no injunctions. Hence, just as the assertion, тАШRice forms the food of Kings,тАЩ is regarded as a mere praise of the Rice, and it is not taken us a prohibition of its eating by people other than Kings,тАФin the same manner, in the present passage the phrase тАШnot by any one elseтАЩ is not a prohibition (of study by others), but only a praise of the institutes; the sense thus is as follows:тАФ

тАШThe Br─Бhmaс╣Зa is the highest being in the world,тАФthese institutes are the best of all institutes,тАФhence these are capable of being studied and taught only by the said learned Br─Бhmaс╣Зa,тАФand they cannot be either studied or taught by any ordinary man.тАЩ It is in view of this that the author adds the term тАШwith great careтАЩ; unless great care is taken, until the self has been duly equipped with the knowledge of other sciences,тАФsuch as Logic, Grammar and Exigetics,тАФthese institutes cannot be taught. If the present verse were taken as an injunction of тАШstudy,тАЩ the said тАШlearningтАЩ could he regarded only as serving some transcendental purpose. It would not he right to argue thatтАФтАЬin the injunction also hearing would be implied by the studyingтАЭ; for it is not right to take what is enjoined as subserving the purposes of implications.
