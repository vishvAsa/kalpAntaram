  1. Jitendriya Jitendriya is one of those writers who at one time held an eminent position but in course of time sank into unmerited oblivion. The works of Jimūtavāhana bear abundant testi mony to the fact that Jitendriya wrote an extensive work on dharmaśāstra. In his Kālaviveka (p. 380 ) Jimūta vāhana says that Jitendriya797 wrote on the topic of kāla (i. e. on determining doubtful points about the months, the tithis, samkrāntis, &c, and the religious rites to be performed on them ). In the Vyava hāramātṇkā of Jimātavāhana also Jitendriya’s views are cited (on pp. 302, 334 ). This shows that he wrote also on pro cedure in law courts. Jitendriya is also referred to in the Dāyatattva of Raghunandana.804 But no other early writer quotes Jitendriya. Therefore it appears that Jitendriya was probably a Bengal writer and flourished about 1000-1050 A. D. and that he was completely eclipsed by the brilliant Jimutavāhana.
