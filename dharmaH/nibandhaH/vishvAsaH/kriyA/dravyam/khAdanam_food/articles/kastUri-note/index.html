Protein, ahimsA, sAttvika context

A note on H diet discourse:
Against my initial reluctance to write anything on this topic, a discussion with Twitter moot, the incoherent response from H paksha to the attack on their diet choices by anti-H, ul̤pāvādais, (in-skirts as opposed to pāvādais/skirts) pretending to be H, edge lords and the like pretending to care about protein deficiency and nutrition needs of Indians. The following is my own opinion and caveat emptor.

The issue is posited as protein deficiency and a suggestion that meat, eggs or fish must be consumed by H. The sectarian sampradaya autists, believers of Quixotic unscientific concepts (otherwise intelligent) and the like offer confused defenses like

  • (a) we avoid non-sattvic food (unscientific) and
  • (b) we want to reduce ahimsā (morality based).

With (a), the interlocutor will make you look like a fool from he high horse of scientism. “You believe in nonsense like 3 guṇas? What are you an Oakist?”.

With (b), “What about eggs? They don’t hatch into chickens and you’re not killing anything.”.

Counter arguments will again be unscientific. Here’s how I would approach.

With Anti-H

First establish baseline. If the interlocutor is plain anti-H, never go into śāstras or Oakisms. Agree with the protein requirement and take it to the extreme. Go in attack mode and escalate step by step.

“Deshis are indeed protein deficient. How about opening nationwide eateries serving bow-bow biriyani, kabootar kofta curry, caw-caw kadai masala, lababdar Labrador, baked billi pie, chooha burger? We solve dogesh menace haunting the pedestrians, kabootar pests and caw caw mischief makers”. You kill 2 birds (and dogs, pigeons, crows, rats and other pests).

There should be meat markets of dogesh bhais hanging but it should only be cooked by approved appliances to ensure health safety."

If this hits a nerve on them, they have their own morality boundaries. Your response should be

“Why should your morality boundary be honored over mine? So science or protein deficiency isn’t your concern but needling H is. Just say that you’re a anti-H bigot and move on”.

No dialog with an anti-H bigot is possible, let alone dietary choice justification. If they agree for argument sake, if the opponent agrees, escalate to human meat. If there’s no morality boundaries, anything goes no? If the opponent still agrees, just agree. Neither of us will really like to live in a reality without some moral boundaries and this is just a thought experiment in futility.

With well intentioned H

If the interlocutor is indeed a good intentioned H, is confused by proteinmaxxing jeets and the respondent is a sectarian sampradaya autist, my suggestion would be for the respondent to tone down and not go into an attack mode. Assume good intention. In a sane world (and perhaps on the ground in reality), this shouldn’t even be a problem.

bali

Vast majority of devout H are already meat eaters but are well aware of religious norms/ācāra. They consume meat only on certain days, never visit temple on those days or perform religious rituals. I personally know a family of advija friends who ritually offer goats to their village deity with multi-day celebration. The terminal day involves cooking of dal/rice ritually on the temple premises and the bali of the goats. They actually observe various vratas and avoid meat for a specific period leading to the bali day.

With government clamping down on bali in temples, they workaround the issue by taking the aja, getting it washed by the priest and take the aja to an adjacent open ground, slaughter and eat it.

What is understood subconsciously, just needs to be coherently articulated.

IMO occasionally religious lay H should absolutely focus on good nutrition and health. Go ahead and consume milk, eggs, allowed meat, fish and the like.

Before a religious event, observe fast, consume purificatory foods, finish the event and return to business as usual. This is what is practiced on the ground (at least from my observation).

The following is an attempt to present a coherent H paksha for H audience only. It will be from a dharmaśāstra point of view of dvijas but core points are same for any H following their own kulācāra.

abhojya - arbitrary norms

1. We have our own concepts of abhakṣya and abhojya, (amedya, apeya etc. are similar concepts). Food that is inherently forbidden and food that is conditionally forbidden. Examples of the latter are hair or insects falling into the food (pollution), milk from an animal for 10 days after it gives birth (compassion).

Consult dharmaśāstra and social norm to establish non-negotiables, a line that should not be crossed. From the protein argument, we have already established that moral boundaries of different groups are inherently arbitrary. This is what forms the baseline for us - not ahimsā or pseduo-scientific sattvic food theories.

dvijas and sAdhanA

2. These rules matter mainly for dvijas who are constantly engaged in their ordained karmans or mantra sadhana.

This group would be a micro-minority and violating the abhojya rules is our internal matter. Our duty to perform rituals entail maintaining a pure ritual space and ritual implements that include one’s own body.

In that context avoiding ritually polluting food is a mandatory requirement. This requirement extends to everyone who enters the ritual space divjas or advijas. It is a social contract between various participants to maintain the ritual requirements in the shared space.

3. The 3 guṇa theory is unscientific but the practitioners know there’s a difference in the quality of sadhana or karma when one adheres to all the rules. Instead of arguing the indefensible, we can at least suggest this theory as a modeling tool.

Netflix learns from your watch history and preferences and categorizes you to present targeted ads or recommend suggested content to view next. The guṇa theory could similarly be a modeling tool that could be built based on the patient’s dietary practice, exercise habits, gut microbiome profile, place of dwelling etc.

We can empirically test if this data can indeed model across 3 dimensions and the guṇa is simply the dimensional label here. Not a scientific truth claim. What you eat can affect your gut and your gut health can have neurological effects shouldn’t be controversial at all.

We can even test if adding more dimensions should be added and we have positively contributed to the progression of H thoughts.

Controlled transgression

4. IMO even practicing dvijas should optimize for health and maybe break abhojya rules in a controlled manner. Dietary restrictions have evolved into insane levels - avoid this vegetable, avoid that fruit, avoid these grains/pseudo-grains/beans.

Perform trisuparna rite of aghamarshana rite for prāyaścitta. I wouldn’t probably go as far as a state requiring kūśmāṇḍa.(I’m being little bit hypocritical and I do largely follow the rules. The price I pay is a large set of supplements which should ideally come from my food.)

Definitely consult your doctor/ and assess macro and micronutrient deficiency. The basics are “have your food from a variety of sources”, “avoid processed foods, polished grains”, “combine grains and legumes and beans”, “dairy products are good but be aware of fat intake”, “don’t neglect micronutrients”.

No poking

  1. Don’t poke your nose in the kulācāra of a different H jāti. Again, you have your morality boundary and they have theirs. /End-rant
