I can understand where you are coming from. I have seen my fair share of bad priests and as someone who has a deep appreciation of these matters, it is very disheartening. So do trust me when I say I understand this.

At the same time, I think we should avoid conflating the caliber of a priest with how “cheap” he is. Not saying you are doing so but this is something that has been on my mind and I feel the need to address it. I do feel that many H find it painful to give good Dakṣiṇā to priests. 

Firstly, it is counterintuitive to want a good priest to be “too cheap”. It reinforces the thinking that these rituals are of no value. And that’s a bad vāsana to reinforce. 

Secondly, I think H should want their priests to do well in life. 

Perhaps, this is my svabhāva. But even when I was young and didn’t have much, I had never liked the “Great Indian/Asian heritage of bargaining”. 

And I think this kind of thinking should at least never have a place in religious matters because it injures the state of mind in both the priest and the Yajamāna.
